Why these translations?

This project compares three translations selected for how differently they approach the words of Jesus. Each reflects a distinct translation philosophy, making their differences intentional and meaningful.

DBH (David Bentley Hart, 2023)

A highly literal and philosophically precise translation from the Greek, preserving structure and nuance often smoothed out in modern versions.

NRSVUE (2021)

A modern scholarly standard, balancing accuracy and readability while incorporating updated manuscript evidence and translation practices.

LAMSA (George M. Lamsa, 1957)

Translated from the Aramaic Peshitta tradition, offering a different linguistic and cultural perspective on the text.

Seen side by side, they reveal where wording aligns, where it diverges, and how meaning becomes clearer through contrast.
